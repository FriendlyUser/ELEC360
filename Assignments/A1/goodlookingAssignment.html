  1. \(t^2\) By using the definition of Laplace Transform and integration by parts, \(\mathscr{L}[t^2]\) can be found. \[\begin{aligned} & \mathscr{L}[t^2] = \int_{0}^{\infty} t^2 e^{-st} {\mathop{}\!d}t \\ & \int_{0}^{\infty} t^2 e^{-st} {\mathop{}\!d}t = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{2} u &= t^2 \quad & {\mathop{}\!d}v &= e^{-st} {\mathop{}\!d}t \\ {\mathop{}\!d}u &= 2t {\mathop{}\!d}t \quad & v &= \frac{-1}{s}e^{-st} \end{alignedat}\, \right] = \cancelto{0}{\left[ \frac{-1}{s}t^2e^{-st} \right]^{\infty}_{0}} - \int_{0}^{\infty}2t\frac{-e^{-st}}{s} {\mathop{}\!d}t \\ &\frac{2}{s}\int_{0}^{\infty}t e^{-st} {\mathop{}\!d}t = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{2} u_2 &= t \quad & {\mathop{}\!d}v_2 &= e^{-st} {\mathop{}\!d}t \\ {\mathop{}\!d}u_2 &= {\mathop{}\!d}t \quad & v_2 &= \frac{-1}{s}e^{-st} \end{alignedat}\, \right] = \frac{2}{s}\left( \cancelto{0}{\left[ \frac{-t}{s}e^{-st} \right]^{\infty}_{0}} + \frac{1}{s}\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-st} {\mathop{}\!d}t \right) \\ & \frac{2}{s} \int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-st} {\mathop{}\!d}t = \frac{2}{s^3} \\ & \boxed{\mathscr{L}[t^2] = \frac{2}{s^3}, \quad s > 0} \end{aligned}\]

  2. \(\sin(\omega t) = \frac{1}{2j}\left( e^{j\omega t}+ e^{-j\omega t}\right)\) \[\begin{aligned} & \mathscr{L} \left[\frac{1}{2j}\left( e^{j\omega t}+ e^{-j\omega t}\right)\right] = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{2j}\left( e^{j\omega t}- e^{-j\omega t}\right) e^{-st} {\mathop{}\!d}t \\ & = \frac{1}{2j} \left(\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{j\omega t -st} {\mathop{}\!d}t - \int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-j\omega t-st} {\mathop{}\!d}t \right) = \frac{1}{2j} \left. \left(\frac{1}{j\omega -s}e^{j\omega t -st} \right|_{0}^{\infty} -\left. \frac{-1}{j\omega +s} e^{-j\omega t -st} \right|_{0}^{\infty} \right) \\ &= \frac{1}{2j} \left[\frac{1}{s-j\omega}+\frac{1}{s+j\omega}\right] \\ &=\frac{1}{2j}\left(\frac{s+j\omega -s +j\omega}{s+\omega^2}\right)= \frac{1}{2j}\left(\frac{2j\omega}{s+\omega^2}\right) \\ & \boxed {\mathscr{L} \left[\frac{1}{2j}\left( e^{j\omega t}+ e^{-j\omega t}\right)\right] = \frac{\omega}{s+\omega^2}, s > 0} \end{aligned}\]
